Electronics

In this experiment, a 555 IC was used which is a two-state device. This means that the input in pin 3 can only be HI or LO. In this case, a LED is used turned on and off because of the square pulses emitted by Pin 3 and are called “clock blink pulses.” The LED blinked on and off at a frequency of about one time per second. The electrons flow from the negative terminal to the capacitor, then through Pin 2 and through the resistor of 220 Ohms, the LED then to the positive terminal.
